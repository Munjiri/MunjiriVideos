CHARITY VIDEO PRODUCTION

We create videos that show the real and human side to your charity and make audiences stop and take action.

If you are looking for a videographer for your charity, to create awareness and reach fundraising goals with a charity promo video we would love to work with you. We have worked with both larger charities like the Obama Foundation and Trocaire as well as smaller charities like Impact Network and the Pollination Project. 


From our clients we've learnt that commissioning a videographer can be a bit of a daunting task, it's difficult to decide what to include. Will I be posting my video on YouTube? Do I need a script? What about the music? We are happy to guide you through the process so you can feel relaxed and have fun.

IMPACT NETWORK

This video for education charity Impact Network was shown at their charity fundraising event and brought in a high level of donations for the inspiring work they do. Each year, Impact Network serves around 6,000 students across rural Zambia, and provides them with a quality education. These are some of the stories from the community members they partner with.

AFRICAN BAT CONSERVATION

The charity African Bat Conservation conducts applied research, conservation and education to bring bats to the conservation agenda and help conserve bat populations in Africa. Mostly we hear stories about human wildlife conflict, but just outside Lilongwe these bats are helping to create an excellent banana harvest. 

Close

CCPM MALAWI

For this charity video we partnered with the Climate Challenge Programme Malawi, a Scottish Government initiative helping rural communities in Malawi find innovative solutions to problems caused by climate change. Working through local partner organisations, the programme started with a series of workshops in rural communities in southern Malawi, to learn about the problems they face and what would help them to improve their access to food, water and energy.

POLLINATION PROJECT

Pollination Project give small grants every single day to social change-makers all over the world. We visited one of their partners in Nairobi, the very inspiringDavid Mulo who started the sport for development organization Green Kenya. David and his team work towards creating opportunities and building resilience in young people who live in challenging environments.
